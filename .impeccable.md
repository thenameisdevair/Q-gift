# QGift — Design Context

QGift is a MiniPay mini app on Celo where someone sends a friend a cUSD gift wrapped in an occasion and a written message. The product is a 60-second flow: see the prompt → write the note → pick the amount → tap send. The smart contract (`QGift.sol`, deployed on Celo mainnet) emits `GiftSent(sender, recipient, amount, occasion, message)` and that on-chain memory is the artifact.

This file is the source of truth for every design decision in this repo. Anything not in here is a defaulted choice and should be questioned.

---

## Design Context

### Users

The primary user is **18–35, in Nigeria, Kenya, or Ghana, on MiniPay**. They are sending to someone they have a real relationship with — a friend, a sibling, a partner — usually for a real occasion (birthday, congratulations, "thinking of you"). They are not making a remittance and they are not power crypto users. They are mobile-first by necessity (low-end Android, variable data) and culturally fluent.

The moment of use is **deliberate but quick**. They remembered it's the friend's birthday, they want to send something that feels like more than a Venmo. They have ~60 seconds before they get distracted. The interface has to *deserve* those 60 seconds — make them feel like they did something nicer than tapping "send" — without spending any of those seconds on its own ego.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **professional · friendly · cool**.

That triad is unusual on purpose. "Professional" rules out childishness, balloon graphics, and confetti. "Friendly" rules out fintech coldness and crypto seriousness. "Cool" rules out a Hallmark card. The intersection is the brand: a quietly confident, warm, modern celebration product. Think a really well-designed birthday card from a Lagos creative studio. Think a tasteful party invitation that reads like editorial design. Bright but never loud, festive but never chaotic.

Emotional goals — what the sender feels: *I did this right*. *This was easy and felt nice*. *I'd use this again next month*.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme: dark.** A deep, near-black background with a subtle cool/teal undertone — celebration worth opening, not bank software. Light mode is explicitly excluded; it would push the product toward Paystack clinical and lose the differentiator.

**Palette (OKLCH, neutrals tinted toward the primary's hue family):**

```
--bg            oklch(0.155 0.012 175)   /* deep teal-tinted near-black */
--surface       oklch(0.205 0.014 175)   /* card / elevated */
--surface-2     oklch(0.255 0.014 175)   /* hover / pressed surface */
--border        oklch(0.32 0.014 175)    /* hairline 1px only */
--text          oklch(0.97 0.006 175)    /* near-white, slight tint */
--text-muted    oklch(0.72 0.012 175)
--text-soft     oklch(0.58 0.014 175)
--ice-tea       oklch(0.88 0.058 168)    /* primary ~ #B5E8D5, soft mint-teal */
--ice-tea-ink   oklch(0.32 0.06  168)    /* ink-on-ice for buttons */
--gold          oklch(0.85 0.13  86)     /* warm celebratory yellow, not neon */
--gold-deep     oklch(0.72 0.135 78)     /* gold pressed/active */
```

**60-30-10:** 60% bg + surface, 30% text + hairlines + muted, 10% accent (ice-tea OR gold per screen, almost never both at full strength). Ice-tea is the *system* accent (CTAs, focus, primary surfaces); gold is the *occasion* accent (the gift moment itself, the on-confirmation flourish, key numerals). Using both at once dilutes both — pick one per surface.

**Typography (banned reflexes excluded — no Inter, DM, Plex, Outfit, Instrument, Fraunces, Cormorant, etc.):**

- **Display: Boska** (ITF / Fontshare, free for commercial). High-contrast modern serif with warmth and editorial confidence — the "expensive birthday card" face. Used for the gift's dollar amount, the recipient name, and the occasion title.
- **Body: Switzer** (ITF / Fontshare, free for commercial). Variable grotesque designed to pair with Boska, same foundry. Quietly characterful, not the General Sans / Geist default.

Self-host both via `next/font` from local `.woff2` (no Google Fonts CDN — saves DNS+TLS hops in MiniPay WebView and respects the 2MB bundle cap). Subset to Latin only.

Modular scale, fixed `rem` (this is a product UI, not a marketing site): **0.75 / 0.875 / 1 / 1.25 / 1.625 / 2.25 / 3.5** rem with **1.3** ratio at the high end for impact on the gift amount.

**Anti-references (hard):**
- ❌ Crypto wallet UI (no chain badges, no "0x..." anywhere unless explicitly debug)
- ❌ Cash App / Venmo / Paystack clinical look
- ❌ Purple-blue gradients, neon-on-dark, glowing accents
- ❌ Ankara / kente / mudcloth prints — culturally literal, design-flat
- ❌ Confetti, balloons, party-emoji decoration — childish, undermines "professional"
- ❌ Gradient text, `background-clip: text` — banned outright
- ❌ Side-stripe `border-left` accent on cards — banned outright
- ❌ The shadcn template hero (badge → big heading → subtitle → CTA) — currently in `apps/web/src/app/page.tsx`, must be replaced

### Design Principles

1. **Celebrate, don't transact.** Every screen should feel like opening a card, not approving a payment. The word "transaction" never appears in UI copy. The amount is shown in display serif at the gift moment, not in tabular monospace. The on-chain message is presented like writing — not a memo field.
2. **One generous gesture per screen.** The 60-second flow has three real moments: write, choose amount, send. Each screen does one of those things memorably and leaves everything else off. No combined forms, no "step 2 of 7", no progress bars (those belong in onboarding, not gifts).
3. **Type carries the emotion.** With a 2MB bundle, no animation library, and a dark monochrome base, the only place left for personality is type. Boska does the celebrating; Switzer gets out of the way. Numbers (the gift amount) are display, not tabular.
4. **Pan-African polish, not pan-African pastiche.** The cultural fluency comes from craft, color, and rhythm — not from imagery that flattens. The product feels designed by someone *from* Lagos/Nairobi/Accra, not designed *about* them.
5. **Lean by default, motion only with purpose.** CSS transitions only — `transform`, `opacity`, `grid-template-rows` for height. One choreographed entrance per screen (staggered, 220–360ms, ease-out-quart), one press-feedback transition (120ms), one confirmation flourish on send. Nothing else moves. No skeleton shimmer. No decorative parallax. `prefers-reduced-motion` collapses everything to instant.

### Constraints (hard)

- **Viewport:** mobile-first, 375–414px primary, 360px absolute minimum. Tablet/desktop is "make it center and don't break"; not a layout target.
- **Performance:** low-end Android, variable data — initial route under 2MB transferred including fonts. No heavy libs (no framer-motion, no GSAP, no lottie). CSS only.
- **MiniPay WebView:** wallet **auto-connects** — there is no Connect button anywhere in the app. The current `connect-button.tsx` should be removed or repurposed for a non-MiniPay fallback only. Use `eth_sendTransaction` legacy txns (no EIP-1559) per MiniPay spec.
- **Stack:** Tailwind stays. shadcn primitives are optional — keep `Button` and the `cn()` util, drop `Sheet`/`Card` if they cost more than they earn for two pages. Lucide icons are fine but use ≤6 across the whole app and tree-shake by importing per-icon.
- **Accessibility:** color contrast ≥ AA on every text-on-surface combination including ice-tea-on-bg and gold-on-bg (will need a dark ink variant for primary CTAs). Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`. Tap targets ≥ 44px. No motion-only feedback.
